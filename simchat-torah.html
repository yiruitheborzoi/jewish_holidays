Simchat Torah

Simchat Torah, meaning “Rejoicing with the Torah,” celebrates the completion of the annual cycle of Torah readings and immediately begins the cycle anew. It follows Sukkot and Shemini Atzeret.

Overview

Outside Israel, Simchat Torah is celebrated on the second day of Shemini Atzeret. The festival marks both the completion and the immediate restart of the Torah reading cycle, highlighting the continuous engagement with sacred texts.

Customs & Traditions

In synagogues, congregants parade the Torah scrolls around the sanctuary in joyful processions called hakafot, singing and dancing. The concluding verses of Deuteronomy are read, followed by the opening of Genesis, symbolizing the unbroken cycle of study. Children often receive flags or treats to join the celebration.

Significance

Simchat Torah expresses love and joy for the Torah. By immediately beginning the reading again, the festival reinforces that learning and spiritual growth are ongoing and never truly complete.
