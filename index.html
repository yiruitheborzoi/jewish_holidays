Overview

Welcome to the Jewish Holidays website. Here, you can explore the history, traditions, and celebrations of Jewish holidays. Learn about their significance and how they are observed.

Holidays

Rosh Hashanah

Date Range: 5 Sep to 5 Oct

Rosh Hashanah marks the Jewish New Year. It is celebrated with prayer, reflection, and festive meals.

Yom Kippur

Date Range: 14 Sep to 14 Oct

Known as the Day of Atonement, Yom Kippur is marked by fasting and prayer.

Sukkot

Date Range: 19 Sep to 19 Oct

Sukkot commemorates the sheltering of the Israelites in the wilderness.

Shemini Atzeret

Date Range: 26 Sep to 26 Oct

A time of spiritual reflection and prayer marking the end of Sukkot.

Simchat Torah

Date Range: 27 Sep to 27 Oct

Celebrates the conclusion and restart of the Torah reading cycle.

Hanukkah

Date Range: 28 Nov to 27 Dec

Hanukkah celebrates the rededication of the Second Temple in Jerusalem.

Tu Bishvat

Date Range: 15 Jan to 14 Feb

Known as the New Year for Trees, celebrating nature and planting.

Purim

Date Range: 24 Feb to 26 Mar

Purim celebrates the saving of the Jewish people from Haman in the Book of Esther.

Passover

Date Range: 26 Mar to 25 Apr

Passover commemorates the liberation of the Israelites from slavery in Egypt.

Shavuot

Date Range: 15 May to 14 Jun

Shavuot celebrates the giving of the Torah at Mount Sinai.

Tisha B'Av

Date Range: 16 Jul to 15 Aug

A day of mourning to commemorate various disasters in Jewish history.
