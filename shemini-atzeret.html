Shemini Atzeret

Shemini Atzeret, meaning “Assembly of the Eighth Day,” follows immediately after Sukkot and marks a pause to linger in the joy of the festival. It is observed on the 22nd of Tishri.

Overview

This one‑day festival (two outside Israel) comes directly after the seven days of Sukkot. Unlike Sukkot, it has no special dwelling requirements or rituals with the four species. It offers a sacred pause, as if the divine host invites guests to stay one more day before parting.

Customs & Traditions

On Shemini Atzeret, Jews no longer wave the lulav and etrog but may still eat in the sukkah. Synagogue services include reciting Yizkor, the memorial prayer for departed loved ones, and the prayer for rain (Tefilat HaGeshem), invoking blessing for the coming season.

Significance

Following the universal themes of Sukkot, this festival highlights the intimate relationship between God and the Jewish people. It reinforces gratitude for life‑giving rain and encourages worshippers to carry forward the spiritual elevation of the High Holidays into daily life.
