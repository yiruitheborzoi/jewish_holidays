Tisha B'Av

Tisha B'Av, the Ninth of Av, is a day of fasting and mourning that commemorates the destruction of the First and Second Temples and other tragedies throughout Jewish history.

Overview

Falling in mid‑summer, Tisha B'Av concludes a three‑week period of mourning that begins with the fast of the 17th of Tammuz. It recalls multiple national calamities, including the destructions of both Temples in Jerusalem.

Customs & Traditions

From sunset to nightfall the next day, observers refrain from eating, drinking, bathing, wearing leather shoes and other comforts. The Book of Lamentations (Eicha) is read by candlelight, and elegies (kinot) are chanted. Mourners sit on low stools or on the floor to express grief. Work is minimized and joyous activities are avoided.

Significance

Tisha B'Av provides a collective space for grief, reflection and repentance. By confronting historical losses, the day encourages hope for future redemption and a commitment to building a more just and compassionate world.
