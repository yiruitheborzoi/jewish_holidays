Tu BiShvat

Tu BiShvat, the New Year of the Trees, is a mid‑winter festival that celebrates nature, growth and renewal on the 15th day of the month of Shevat.

Overview

Tu BiShvat is one of four “New Years” in the Jewish calendar. Originally used to determine the age of fruit trees for tithing purposes, it has evolved into a celebration of the natural world and ecological awareness.

Customs & Traditions

In Israel, many people plant trees and participate in environmental projects. A Kabbalistic tradition known as the Tu BiShvat seder involves eating specific fruits and nuts—often in four courses accompanied by white and red wine—to symbolize the cycles of nature. Many enjoy a variety of dried fruits, nuts and fresh produce to honor the land.

Significance

Tu BiShvat encourages appreciation for the earth and reminds Jews of their connection to the Land of Israel. By focusing on trees and fruits, the holiday underscores themes of growth, sustainability and stewardship of the environment.
