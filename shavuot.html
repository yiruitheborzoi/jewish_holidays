Shavuot

Shavuot, the Festival of Weeks, occurs seven weeks after Passover. It celebrates the giving of the Torah at Mount Sinai and the wheat harvest in ancient Israel.

Overview

Shavuot, also called the Feast of Weeks, marks the culmination of the seven‑week Counting of the Omer. It commemorates the moment when the Israelites received the Ten Commandments and became a covenantal people.

Customs & Traditions

Many stay up all night studying Torah in a practice called Tikkun Leil Shavuot. The Book of Ruth is read in synagogue, linking the harvest season to themes of loyalty and conversion. Dairy foods like cheesecake and blintzes are enjoyed, and homes and synagogues may be adorned with greenery and flowers.

Significance

This festival celebrates revelation and the acceptance of divine law. It emphasizes the importance of study, community and gratitude for both spiritual and physical nourishment.
